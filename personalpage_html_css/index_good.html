Who Am I?

My name is Neil Borle and I'm a 4th year computing science student in the specialization of bioinfomatics. I started writing software in my first year of study at the University of Alberta and got hooked after that. I still really enjoy studying biology, so I've found a way to combine these two passions. Before starting university I also developed a love of music and of soccer.

Professional History

My first software job was working in the Department of Mathematics at the University of Alberta during the summer of 2011. Since then, I've held two summer NSERC USRAs with the Department of Computing Science where I worked on python development in a webrowser and SkyPython respectively. Most recently (2014), through the Department of Computing Science, I've held a one year work internship with EMC Corporation to develop my professional software skills. During that time I maintained an existing test framework and started development of a new framework.

Qualities

From my internship and past work experiences, I've developed a deep knowledge of Python and a strong understanding of software practices. I consistently made sure that I was present for core work hours at EMC and have had the privilege of working with many different and talented people. I think my strongest asset is that I understand what it takes to deliver a project with the constraints of a professional environment.
